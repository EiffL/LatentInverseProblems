# Latent Diffusion Inversion Requires Understanding the Latent Space
**Authors:** Rao, Qu, Moyer
**Year:** 2025 | **Venue:** arXiv
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20592

## Key idea (1-2 sentences)
Decoder Jacobian has decaying singular values, creating anisotropic latent dimensions. Isotropic guidance is suboptimal.

## Method summary (≤10 lines)
Analyzes SVD of decoder Jacobians in Stable Diffusion. Singular value spectrum decays rapidly. Many latent dims have negligible pixel effect. Proposes Jacobian-aware preconditioning.

## Relevance to our problem
Latent MMPS naturally accounts for Jacobian anisotropy via J·J^T in the covariance, which may explain its improvement over isotropic DPS.

## Key equations
SVD: J_D = U Σ V^T; Effective dimension: d_eff = (Σ_i σ_i)² / Σ_i σ_i²

## Limitations noted by authors
Analysis on Stable Diffusion only.

## Experimental takeaway
Jacobian-aware methods consistently outperform isotropic ones.
